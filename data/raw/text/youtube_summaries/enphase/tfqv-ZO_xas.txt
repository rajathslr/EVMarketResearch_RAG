**Introduction to System Settings and Controls**
- The system features both **"set it and forget it" options and granular controls** targeted toward clean energy optimization.
- The **System page** offers detailed insights into device status and system performance.
- The **"Know Your System"** section provides access to the latest topics, videos, and articles through the nPhase community, ideal for users seeking deeper understanding or updates.
- The **Site Details** area allows users to view installer contact info for support purposes.

**Generating Reports and Device Management**
- Users can generate reports by tapping the **Reports** tab, selecting the report type, setting parameters, and using the **Email Report** button to receive system reports via registered email.
- The **Devices** page lists all system components, providing detailed information on each.
- Some devices allow configuration changes directly via the hardware settings accessible on this page.
- For storage systems, live status and **Live Vitals** data (e.g., IQ System Controller and individual IQ batteries) are accessible to monitor real-time system health.

**Event and Backup History Insights**
- The **Event History** tab logs recorded system events and changes.
- Users can view event specifics such as:
- Start and end dates
- Impacted devices
- Event descriptions
- Recommended actions
- The **Backup History** tab provides details related to system power provision during grid outages. This history is crucial for analyzing system reliability in outage situations.

**Settings Overview and Energy Profiles**
- The **Settings page** allows users to adjust controls on their nPhase system, particularly if they have an IQ battery.
- The **Profile** section supports profile selection aligned to **primary energy goals**:
- **Storm Guard**: Automatically activates full backup during severe weather based on local weather department updates; charges the battery to 100% to ensure power availability during outages.
- **Savings Profile**: Optimizes financial savings by powering the home via battery when electricity prices peak, contingent on entering an electricity rate.
- **Self-Consumption Profile**: Maximizes use of solar energy by storing excess solar power in batteries during the day and powering the home when the sun is down.
- **Full Backup Profile**: Reserves 100% battery power for outages, maintaining battery charge via solar panels as needed.

**Details on Energy Profiles and Battery Behavior**
- **Savings Profile** requires the addition of an electricity rate to function properly; this feature will be explained further in the following segments.
- The **Self-Consumption Profile** is specifically designed to enhance solar energy utilization by balancing battery charging and discharging based on solar availability.
- The **Full Backup Profile** is safety-focused, ensuring the battery maintains full charge as a reserve strictly for outage scenarios.

**Battery Settings Management**
- Users manage battery preferences in the **Battery** section on the Settings page. Features include:
- Ability to **enable or disable battery charging from the grid** per chosen battery profile.
- Configuring a **battery shutdown level percentage** during outages to preserve energy, ensuring batteries can restart effectively when grid power returns or solar production resumes.

**Electricity Rate Configuration for Savings Profile**
- To utilize the Savings Profile and estimate energy production value, users must input electricity rates:
- Access via **Electricity Rate** in settings.
- Add **Electricity Import Rate** using either automatic autofill or manual entry.
- Have the electric bill ready for accurate data entry.
- **Autofill Process:**
- Select utility company and tariff name/code (also known as rate schedule or structure found on the bill).
- Confirm and save the information.
- Users then repeat the process to add the **Electricity Export Rate** for complete rate setup.

**Grid Control Feature**
- The **Grid Control** setting enables manual control to turn the grid connection on or off.
- This is an **advanced feature** and its frequent use is discouraged due to complexity and potential implications.
- When toggling grid connection:
- The system requires verification via a **four-digit code** sent by email and text to associated contacts.
- Upon successful verification, the system disconnects or reconnects to the grid accordingly.

**Summary of System Control Security and Verification**
- Grid connection toggling is secured through multi-factor verification, ensuring only authorized users perform this critical action.
- Notifications come via both email and SMS tied to the registered account to maintain security integrity.
