**Introduction to Enphase App Features**
- Presenter Duncan, Technical Trainer at Enphase, introduces key features of the Enphase App designed to optimize user experience with their solar systems.
- The **"Status" page** is described as the primary interface users will interact with, offering a system overview and data summary for the day.
- Located at the top right is a **"question mark" icon** providing access to various Customer Support options for assistance.
- Just below, users can see the **current weather** conditions relevant to solar generation.

**Real-time System Status and Power Monitoring**
- The displayed data on this page updates every **15 minutes**, ensuring near real-time monitoring.
- Beneath the weather and status, users find **latest power readings from all solar panels**, which naturally vary depending on time and weather.
- **"Peak power"** represents the maximum output recorded from the panels so far during the day.
- The app includes a graph depicting the home’s energy usage through distinct icons:
- **House icon:** Total electricity consumed by the home today.
- **Solar panel icon:** Total electricity generated by the solar panels.
- **Electricity pole icon:** Power imported from the electrical grid.

**Energy Flow and Grid Interaction Explained**
- The app helps users understand energy flow:
- Nighttime typically involves **importing electricity from the grid** due to lack of sunlight.
- During daylight, users may generate excess electricity when household consumption is low. This excess can often be **exported back to the grid**, potentially generating credits on the power bill depending on location and local regulations.
- The app shows the **amount of electricity exported back to the grid**, supporting energy management and cost savings strategies.
- A navigation tab labeled **"Energy"** at the bottom enables users to explore system performance trends over time.

**Energy Trends and Custom Data Analysis**
- The **Energy tab** allows inspection of energy consumption and production data over customizable time frames:
- Standard options such as weekly views or custom dates can align with electricity billing cycles for accurate bill comparison.
- The top of the page displays the **total energy data for the selected period**.
- Below this summary, a detailed graph presents:
- **Solar production (blue line/area)** with expected peaks during midday when sunlight is strongest.
- **Home electricity consumption (orange)** fluctuating throughout the day.
- **Grid import/export (grey)** indicating when electricity is drawn from or sent to the grid.

- This visualization clearly shows typical daily patterns — solar power exported during sunlight hours, with electricity imported at night or when generation is insufficient.

**Panel-level Monitoring with Enphase Microinverters**
- Switching to the **"Array" tab** reveals the granular benefit of **Enphase microinverters**:
- Users can view performance data on a **per individual solar panel basis** rather than aggregate system data.
- Panel color intensity reflects energy output:
- **Lighter colors indicate higher electricity production** for that timeframe.
- **Darker colors suggest reduced output**, often caused by shading or sun position constraints.
- This panel-level insight facilitates rapid identification of underperforming panels or shading effects.

**Settings and Support Features**
- The **"Settings" tab** provides access to important system information, including:
- Full system details and specifications.
- Installer contact information for direct support.
- Additional help resources and support options.
- A **refer-a-friend** feature encouraging network growth and customer engagement.
- Duncan highlights these functionalities as part of the **comprehensive capabilities of the Enphase App** designed to enhance user experience and system maintenance.
