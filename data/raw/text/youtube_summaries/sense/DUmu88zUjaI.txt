**Introduction and Product Overview**
Dale from Renewable Innovations introduces the **Sense energy monitor**, a product he has tracked for years and installed in multiple California homes. Sense distinguishes itself from other energy monitors by not requiring a current transformer (CT) clamped on every circuit. Instead, it uses **high-resolution CTs** that measure electrical draw down to the millisecond.

- **Key technology:**
- Uses **machine learning** to analyze unique electrical signatures of each device
- Identifies individual home appliances such as coffee makers, microwaves, TVs, and air conditioners
- **Benefit for homeowners:**
- Eliminates guesswork about electricity bills
- Provides clarity on which devices contribute most to energy consumption

**How Sense Works and Its Core Advantages**
Sense analyzes the electrical current at a highly granular level, leveraging the fact that every device emits a **unique electrical signature**. This allows the system to identify loads with only a few CT sensors installed. The accompanying app helps track and report this data to the user, enabling fine-grained insights into consumption patterns.

- **Use cases:**
- Detects excessive air conditioner use
- Identifies lights left on for extended periods
- Detects unusual power draws (e.g., potential power theft from neighbors)

**Sense Solar Model**
For users with older solar setups lacking integrated monitoring, Sense offers a **solar model** that delivers comprehensive home energy metrics. This includes:

- Whole-home consumption
- Grid energy import
- Solar energy production
- Exported solar energy (for utility credits)

Additional features allow users to receive **alerts for solar underperformance**, helping owners monitor their solar investments better.

**Recent Innovations: Sense Flex Model**
Sense has introduced **Sense Flex**, which brings two major enhancements targeting specific consumer needs:

| Feature                      | Description                                                                                                            |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Generator Monitoring**     | Tracks output of portable backup generators, showing real-time load and preventing overloads during power outages.     |
| **400 Amp Split-panel Support** | Supports monitoring for modern homes with 400 amp electrical service split into two 200 amp sub-panels, common in electric homes with EV chargers, heat pumps, induction cooktops, and electric water heaters.  |

- **Importance:**
- Provides users clarity on generator usage during outages
- Enables comprehensive monitoring for large, modern electric service setups that previously lacked good monitoring solutions

**Technical Details of Installation and Operation**
- The Sense monitor consists of small CT clips that attach to electrical lines to measure current ($I$) flowing through these circuits.
- Powered by a dedicated circuit breaker that monitors voltage ($V$), the system captures real-time data combining current and voltage to analyze power consumption accurately.
- The device uses this data to identify load signatures and communicate metrics via the app.

**Limitations with Solar and Battery Storage**
- Sense currently faces challenges when integrating with systems combining both solar panels and home battery storage.
- The difficulty lies in **differentiating power flow**, specifically:
- When power is drawn from solar panels during the day
- When power is supplied from battery discharges at night
- The Sense development team is actively working to improve accuracy in detecting these separate energy sources.
- For homes without home batteries, the solar monitoring model functions effectively immediately after installation.

**Installation Process and Availability**
- Installation is designed to be straightforward and typically takes a few hours for most users.
- Dale offers personal installation assistance in Southern California.
- Installation involves attaching CT sensors, connecting the device to a panel breaker, and completing app setup.

**Summary of Benefits and Use Cases**
- **Sense Energy Monitor** is positioned as a **cost-effective, high-value tool** for:
- Reducing high electric bills by identifying waste
- Maximizing the value and performance of solar systems
- Safely monitoring backup power generators
- Supporting complex home electrical systems (400 amp service panels and sub-panels feeding accessory dwelling units or ADUs)

- The device provides **visibility and control** over home energy usage that other monitors often cannot deliver.

### Glossary and Definitions

| Term                       | Definition                                                                                                                       |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CT (Current Transformer)** | A sensor that clips onto an electrical line to measure the current flowing through that wire.                                     |
| **Electrical Signature**   | Unique pattern or fingerprint of current drawn by an individual appliance when in operation, allowing identification by algorithms.|
| **400 Amp Split Service Panel** | A modern electrical setup where a large 400 amp service is split into two 200 amp sub-panels for high electrical capacity requirements. |
| **Exported Solar Energy**   | Surplus electricity generated by solar panels that is sent back to the grid, earning credits or compensation from utilities.     |
| **Generator Overload**      | Condition when the power demand exceeds the generator’s output capacity, risking damage or failure.                               |
