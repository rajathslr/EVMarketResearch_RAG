**Introduction and Initial Experience with Sense Energy Monitor**
- Joe from Dissecting DIY introduces the Sense Energy Monitor, emphasizing he has two units because the first was a returned, damaged solar version from Amazon.
- The first unit was unusable due to modifications made by the previous owner (cut wiring).
- Joe contacted Sense via Facebook; the company provided **excellent customer service** with fast responses and shipped a replacement in two days.
- Sense directed Joe to their **active community forum**, which is a rich resource for troubleshooting and user support.

**Sense Solar Version and Smart Meter Limitations**
- The solar version has a **solar connector for real-time solar power monitoring**.
- Most utility companies, including Eversource and National Grid, don’t provide real-time monitoring despite having smart meters (called Metro meters), resulting in slow or no updates through official channels.
- The private sector, represented by Sense, offers **much better real-time energy monitoring solutions**.

**Unboxing of Non-Solar Sense Version and Components Overview**
- The non-solar Sense package includes:
- Sense monitor device
- Wall decals for labeling
- Mounting hardware for the electrical panel
- Instructions
- Key device features:
- An **antenna port** for Wi-Fi communication
- Sensors that clamp around each phase wire (usually black and red wires for a 240V system) to measure voltage and amperage
- A white neutral wire that connects to the neutral busbar
- Optional external wall mounting plate for panels with limited internal space
- Proper clamping around wires is critical; clamping all lines together results in no readings (cancelling currents).

**Installation Preparation and Electrical Requirements**
- Installation requires:
- A **240V breaker** (usually a 20A breaker is adequate)
- Basic electrical panel knowledge or professional electrician assistance
- Red and black wires from the Sense device connect to the breaker poles; the white wire connects to the neutral busbar.
- Joe strongly advises **those unfamiliar with electrical panels avoid DIY installation** due to safety risks (shock, injury, or death).

**Panel Inspection and Installation Setup**
- Joe’s panel was messy due to previous electrician work and the presence of a switch, which complicated the installation.
- Joe planned an **external mount** due to lack of internal space, particularly because of generator lines restricting space inside the panel.
- Important safety step: shutting off the main breaker before installation to cut power.

**Wiring and Mounting Details**
- Installed a 240V breaker to power the Sense monitor.
- Red and black wires inserted into the breaker, white wire connected to the neutral busbar.
- Mounted the Sense externally due to panel constraints, adding cable supports and zip ties for securing wiring and antenna positioning towards Wi-Fi signal.
- Small inconvenience but functional external setup, antenna oriented for optimal wireless communication.

**Sense App Overview and Initial Use**
- The app’s **initial screen shows real-time whole-house energy usage in watts**.
- Usage fluctuates visibly when devices turn on/off—ideal for tracking consumption dynamically.
- The app requires **24-48 hours to begin identifying individual devices** using "bubbles" (a detection and learning feature).
- Real-time tracking validates device power consumption claims, e.g.:
- Joe measured his basement LED can lights at about 7.7 watts each, consistent with manufacturer ratings.
- Additional app features include:
- Historical **usage trends by day, week, month, and year**
- Cost estimations based on user-input electricity rates
- Identification of peak usage events like laundry day

**Device Identification and Learning Limitations**
- The Sense monitor has a **learning curve** and can struggle to consistently identify certain devices.
- Successfully identified devices include:
- Boiler (merged with circulator pump for accuracy)
- Coffee maker
- Dryer
- Oven
- Several heat devices (labeled Heat 3, 4, 5) remain unidentified despite testing various heat sources.
- Merging related devices (e.g., boiler and circulator pump) may improve recognition and simplify monitoring but may lose some granularity if separate data is desired.
- Joe found some **detection inconsistencies and finickiness** in the app, particularly with intermittent or less consistent devices.

**Sense Company Development and User Feedback**
- Sense is actively building their **device identification database**, improving accuracy over time.
- Many users on Amazon and other forums express frustration about slow or incomplete device detection.
- Joe advises that if device-level identification is a primary goal, patience and engagement with the learning process are needed.
- For general real-time whole-house energy monitoring and consumption reduction, Sense is **highly recommended and highly effective**.

**Summary of Installation Requirements and Process**
- Key hardware:
- A **20A 240V breaker** (or lowest available 240V breaker)
- Red and black power wires attach to breaker poles
- White neutral wire connects to neutral busbar
- Two clamping sensors around mains feed wires
- Installation challenges: limited panel space, external mount as workaround
- Setup generally straightforward once the plan is in place
- Initial app setup delivers whole-house monitoring immediately, device learning takes longer.

**Overall Impression of Sense Energy Monitor**
- Joe strongly recommends Sense for:
- Real-time, **whole-house energy usage monitoring**
- Monitoring changes when turning devices on/off
- Validating utility billing and achieving energy savings
- Sense is still evolving; device recognition can be inconsistent but improves over time.
- Joe appreciates the app interface and ability to monitor energy use dynamically, including during energy retrofits and efficiency checks.

**Critique of Utility Company Smart Metering**
- Major utilities like Eversource and National Grid have **not innovated much in customer-facing smart metering**.
- Their meters serve the utilities’ billing purposes but provide poor or no real-time consumer data access.
- Sense bridges this gap by providing **transparent and actionable usage data** directly to consumers via app.
